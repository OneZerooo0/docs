# Organizations and Minor Faction Guidelines

## What is this?

This section contains information regarding the creation of player-made organizations and minor factions.

## Organizations: What Are They?

Organizations are similar to "guilds" or other player-made groups. They are made by players, for players, for organizing roleplay or in-game actions. They may have some unique content in-game for flavor but content should not be excessive without maintainer approval (see the Minor Factions section below.)

#### Content
- Organizations do not need explicit approval - They just need to follow rules of the server (including staying within the setting and established lore.)

- Organizations should refrain from adding content unless they have a significant following of players. Until then, stick to organizing in-game activities and roleplay, building lore, etc. 
    - Organizations that develop enough of a following may have content added depending on how popular they are - If an organization becomes large enough, it may be treated as a minor faction (if given maintainer approval) and have content in-game.
    - This is largely due to the difficulty in getting people to create enough content to warrant an organization becoming a minor faction. Organizations should prove their worth before becoming officialized.

#### Lore
- Lore and theming is largely up to the original creator and community of the organization.
    - We request that organizations follow established lore and setting of Monolith, and are largely original (e.g no Galactic Empire from *Star Wars* or a group of Wizards)


#### Importance
- Organize should first develop a playerbase and unique content before becoming "the leading producer of x" and becoming intermingled with other lore.
    - The world doesn't revolve around you - Start small and as your organization becomes more popular, its relevance will expand.


## Minor Factions

"Minor factions" are organizations or other groups that have established a solid enough lore, development power and player-base to warrant officialization. They are not primary actors for the most part (unlike the TSF and PDV), but they are relevant in terms of having active players and in terms of having sufficient content to justify their officiality. They are supported by maintainers and are treated as part of the game for balancing and development.

- When making an organization, do not expect it to become a minor faction. Minor factions are an edge-case scenario, and an organization needs to be popular and have enough people willing to develop content for organizations to become officialized.

- While Organizations are free-form groups of players, Minor Factions are established in-game and thus are under maintainer jurisdiction. This does not mean the founder does not have say in the direction of the minor faction, but rather that faction content is subject to balancing and further development by other contributors.

### Advisory note

Minor factions are difficult, both for players and maintainers, as they need coordination and enough development to warrant their existence. Organizations need to develop a playerbase before getting content added to provide evidence to maintainers that said organizations will not be abandoned - In other words, organizations need to build faith that they will not collapse through having obvious player support, both in how many people like/play in the organization and how many people are willing to create content for it. (remember: Maintainers are not responsible for keeping your organization updated and unique!)
